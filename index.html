✈ FAST Update Generator — Help

🔑 Logging in

  1. Engineers: use the tabs (FAST EMEA / FAST ASIA / OPS) or the search box to find your name, tap it, then enter your 4-digit PIN.
  2. Biometric login (Face ID / Touch ID / Windows Hello): after your first successful PIN login on a supported device, a banner will offer to enable biometric authentication. Once set up, you can sign in with a single tap — no PIN required. You can enable or disable this at any time from your profile settings.
  3. Forgot your PIN? tap "🔑 Forgot my PIN" and answer your security question (set up the first time you chose a PIN) to set a new one. After 5 incorrect attempts, your profile is locked for 15 minutes for security.
  4. Admin: tap "Admin access" and sign in with the admin email/password.

⚙️ Admin panel

Signing in as Admin opens "Manage Team" directly, where you can:

  • Team members: create new engineer profiles, edit their details, reset a forgotten PIN, force an engineer to choose a new PIN on next login, or delete a profile (this also deletes their projects).
  • Default recipients: email addresses added here appear as locked recipients on every new project's FAST Update table by default.
  • Start Job — fixed recipients: set the locked (blue-tag) recipients that appear on every engineer's Customer Update, Travel, Part Request and FBO drafts.
  • Qatar Executive — extra recipients: set the additional recipients added automatically to the Customer Update and Part Request drafts whenever an engineer ticks the ⭐ Qatar Executive toggle in Start Job.
  • FSR Database: remove outdated Gulfstream Field Service Representative contacts (engineers add new ones themselves from the Start Job panel).

📄 Filling in a project

  1. Step 1 — PDF upload (optional): drop an RFS, Work Order Detail or WCR PDF and tap "Extract data" — aircraft fields and work items are filled in automatically. Uploading a Work Order Detail or WCR after an RFS merges in new work items without overwriting fields you've already filled in.
  2. Steps 2–3 — Aircraft details & work items: check or complete the aircraft information, registration, ICAO, whether this is an RTS or Assist job, A/C hours and cycles, estimated RTS, next flight date, and the list of work items (squawks).
  3. Step 4 — Daily updates: tap "+ Today", "+ Yesterday" or "+ Day before" to add a day. A new day automatically carries over any items already checked on the previous day (with their status), so you only need to update what changed. Check off each work item you worked on and set its status (Carried out, Waiting for part(s), Item MEL'd, Cancelled by customer, or In progress), then add a free-text note. Toggle "A/C RTS" on the day the aircraft returns to service. The progress bar on each day card shows how many items are checked. Ticking or changing an item's status on an earlier day also updates that item on every later day already created, so you don't need to repeat it manually.
  4. Parts (optional): paste an email thread into the text area or upload it as a PDF, then tap "Extract parts from email" — part numbers, serials and descriptions are detected automatically. Review and confirm before they are added to the parts replaced list.
  5. Step 5 — Closing details: job description suffix for the email subject, estimated RTS, next flight, return date and time, and any additional constraints or information.
  6. Step 6 — Onsite engineers & customer: you are automatically listed as Lead Engineer — use the dropdown to reassign it to a colleague if needed. Add up to 2 assisting colleagues. Fill in the customer's name, phone, email, company, and customer code for the FAST Update table footer and for pre-filling GA3671 tags.

✨ AI-assisted text correction

Wherever you see a "✨ Improve text" button (daily update notes, Step 5 constraints), tap it to have AI correct grammar, spelling and clarity. It never invents information or changes technical facts (part numbers, dates, squawk numbers, registrations…), and never applies automatically: you always see the suggestion side-by-side with your original text and must confirm before it replaces anything.

🏷️ GA 3671 — Unserviceable Tag

Generates the official Gulfstream unserviceable tag as a filled, downloadable PDF. Open it either with the 🏷️ button next to a specific row in "Parts replaced" (pre-fills that part's name, P/N and S/N), or with the standalone "🏷️ Generate GA3671 tag (standalone)" button below the parts list if the part isn't in your table yet.

Aircraft model, serial, registration, A/C hours & cycles, GAC Work Order #, and customer company/code/contact/phone/email are pre-filled from Steps 2 and 6, and your Employee ID # is pre-filled from your profile's FN Number. A/C hours and cycles are shared across every GA3671 tag for the same job — enter them once (here or in Step 2) and every subsequent tag you generate for this job will already have them, whether typed manually or applied from a Reply Sync suggestion.

Complete the rest as needed: reason for removal (can be pre-filled by linking a work item), work performed/resolution, fault codes, condition at time of failure, cruise altitude, whether the replacement solved the problem, and the Engine/APU section for engine-related squawks. Tap "Generate filled PDF" to download the completed tag.

🖋️ Signature / Inspection Stamp: upload your stamp or signature once (PNG or JPG) from the tag form — it's saved to your profile and automatically embedded on every GA3671 tag you generate afterwards, in the designated stamp box on the form. Use "🔄 Change" to replace it or "🗑 Remove" to delete it from your profile at any time.

The form stays open until you tap Cancel or Generate — clicking outside it will not close it or lose your entries.

📧 Sending the FAST Update

  1. Tap "Generate FAST Update table" to build the final table — this is exactly what gets sent, in the company's fixed format.
  2. "Copy table" copies it to your clipboard for pasting into any email client.
  3. "Draft in Gmail" creates a ready-to-send Gmail draft directly — requires signing in with your Google account once (separate from your engineer PIN).

🚀 Start Job — Customer Update, Part Request, Travel and FBO emails

The Start Job button appears in the engineer bar once you are logged in. It opens a dedicated panel to configure and generate up to 4 Gmail draft emails in one click — customer introduction, travel booking, parts order, and FBO coordination.

You can also launch Start Job directly from any project card using the 🚀 button — the aircraft details are pre-filled automatically from that project. Use "🔄 Clear & New Job" in the panel header to reset the questionnaire for a new intervention while keeping the aircraft info you've already entered.

✈️ Aircraft info

Fill in the aircraft type, MSN, registration, Work Order number, ICAO airport code, and a brief defect description. These fields populate the subject lines and body of all four draft emails. The ICAO field also pre-fills the Travel "To" destination.

The Customer contact name field is used in the greeting line of the Customer Update email ("Dear [name],"). The work items entered here are also the squawks available for assignment in the parts table and for the per-item troubleshooting questions.

📬 Recipients per draft

Each of the four drafts has its own recipient list. Blue locked tags are fixed recipients set by your admin — you cannot remove them. White tags are extras you add yourself for this job only.

The 👤 FSR dropdown (in the Customer Update recipient block) adds a Gulfstream Field Service Representative's email to both the Customer Update and Part Request recipients. Add a new FSR to the shared database directly from this panel if they aren't listed yet.

The ⭐ Qatar Executive toggle adds a specific set of additional recipients to the Customer Update and Part Request drafts — use it when the operator is Qatar Executive.

Part Request recipients are also adjusted automatically based on your parts list: adding a Tool row adds the Tool Crib address; adding a Consumable row adds the Stores address.

👷 Preparing for a colleague

Toggle "I am preparing this job for a colleague" to change the email tone from first-person to third-person ("Our engineer will be on site…"). Select the colleague's name from the dropdown — their details are used in the email introduction. If the travelling engineer is not yet decided, select "Not decided yet" and a generic placeholder phrase is used. When a colleague is selected, tool rows in the parts table reference their FN number instead of yours for the GUL request.

📧 Customer Update

This email introduces the engineer to the customer and opens communication for the intervention. It includes:

  • A personalised greeting using the customer contact name you entered.
  • A statement of the defect and an action plan — either typed manually in the Action plan field, or a generic line if left blank.
  • Your ETA on site — if filled in, it appears as a sentence. Leave blank or write N/A to omit it. Write UNK for a generic "I will keep you informed" line.
  • A list of questions to include — tick only the ones relevant to this job. Available questions: broker for import, aircraft hours and cycles, PO/WO number, next scheduled flight (pre-filled from the project's "Next Flight" field if available), keys location, FBO name, FBO authorisation email, airside access details, local contact/crew details, troubleshooting already carried out, CAS messages / CMC faults, FHDB downloaded, Techops reply received, self-sourced parts certificates, hand carry approval, order before or after confirmed diagnosis.
  • For troubleshooting questions (CAS, FHDB, Techops, previous troubleshooting): if you tick the question AND select specific work items in the sub-list that appears, the question is asked once per selected squawk — e.g. "Regarding item 2.1 (APU FAIL) — has any troubleshooting been carried out?". If no item is selected, the question is asked generically.
  • Your own saved custom questions — add any recurring question you always ask via the "My custom questions" section (saved permanently to your profile, invisible to other engineers). Tick the ones to include per job.
  • A free-text field for any other one-off question, one per line.
  • Your email signature, built automatically from your profile (name, mobile number, Head-start contact).

✈️ Travel Request

This email asks OPS to book the flight to the aircraft location. Configure it with:

  • From / To / Date — departure city (pre-filled from your profile home base), destination ICAO or city, and desired travel date.
  • Preferred time of departure — Morning, Afternoon, Evening, Night, or Any time. Tap again to deselect.
  • Cabin class — Economy and Business, Economy only, or Business only.
  • Extra baggage — toggle on if carrying tools and needing 2 checked bags.
  • Fake return ticket — toggle on to ask OPS to also issue a dummy return ticket, useful as proof of onward travel for visa/immigration purposes when your actual return date isn't confirmed yet.
  • Frequent flyer numbers — add airline loyalty numbers once (saved permanently to your profile). They appear in the email automatically. You can add or remove airlines at any time.

Tick "Aircraft is at my home base" to skip this draft entirely — it will be marked as Skipped and not created in Gmail.

📦 Part Request

This email requests parts, consumables, and/or tools from the Materials team. Configure it with:

  • Email prefix — choose between PART REQUEST, PARTS REQUEST, PART & TOOL REQUEST, or PARTS & TOOLS REQUEST.
  • Request type — Order (place the order immediately) or Availability only (check stock and lead time before committing).
  • Parts & tools table — add one row per item. For each row, fill in the description, part number, serial number off aircraft (or GUL number for tools), quantity, category (Part / Consumable / Tool), an optional note, and the squawk the item belongs to. Consumable rows disable the serial number field. Tool rows add a GUL reference note field.
  • Squawk assignment per row — each row has a squawk selector. Parts are then grouped by squawk in the email body, making it easier for Materials to match orders to work items. Use the bulk assignment bar (select multiple rows with their checkboxes, then pick a squawk) to assign several parts at once.
  • For Consumables, a "small quantity — avoid DG" checkbox avoids dangerous goods classification for small shipments.
  • Avoid NFF clause — tick to include a sentence asking Materials to favour overhauled or repaired units over new ones, to reduce the risk of No Fault Found returns.
  • Priority warehouse — optionally ask Materials to check a specific warehouse first (Savannah, Farnborough, Amsterdam, Singapore).
  • Certificates, hand carry approval — tick to include the relevant sentences in the email.
  • Shipping address — either "Will be provided shortly" (default) or enter the full FBO address manually.
  • Customer's broker — paste broker contact details if the customer has provided one for import.

⭐ Quick add & 🧪 Sealant lookup

Above the parts table, Quick add shows one-tap chips for your team's frequently used tools and consumables (grouped by Parts/Consumables/Tools, filterable with the search box) — tapping a chip adds it straight to the table. Use "⚙️ Manage list" to add, edit or remove entries; this list is shared with the whole team.

The Sealant lookup panel lets you pick a MIL Spec / application (e.g. fuel tank sealant, firewall sealing) to see the approved part numbers, tick the ones you need, and add them to the parts table in one click. Use its own "⚙️ Manage list" to maintain the shared MIL Spec ↔ P/N reference.

🏢 FBO Coordination

This email contacts the FBO at the aircraft location to introduce the engineer and arrange ground support. Configure it with:

  • Equipment checklist — tick what you need the FBO to provide: GPU (115 Vac / 28 Vdc), Oxygen, Nitrogen (High or Low pressure), Steps / Airstair (standard or engine-height), Hangar space, Lavatory service, High Power Bay. A free-text field lets you add anything else.
  • Co-engineers on site — if other FAST engineers will be present, add their names here. They are listed in the FBO email introduction so the FBO knows who to expect.
  • Optional questions — tick to include: how many days advance notice they need, a note that the intervention is pending authorisation, a request for their parts delivery shipping address, a request to act as importer of record for imported parts, a transport / ground transfer request (reveals an optional planned arrival time field), a request for their access documentation requirements, or a request for their toolbox / equipment documentation requirements. Add further custom questions in the free-text field.
  • Documents to attach — tick which of your personal documents you plan to attach to this email: Passport, Photo ID, IBAC / IS-BAO card, Engineer licence, Company authorisation letter, Toolbox list, Visa. Ticked items appear as a list at the bottom of the FBO email so the FBO knows what you are sending.

📨 Preview, edit & generate

At the bottom of the panel, the Drafts to generate section shows all four emails with their subject lines and recipient summaries. Tap any row to open an inline editable preview — you can manually adjust the subject and body before generating. Tap 🔄 Reset to restore the auto-generated version at any time.

Click Generate Gmail drafts to create all drafts in your Gmail Drafts folder in one click. You will be prompted to sign in with Google if not already connected. The Travel draft is skipped automatically if you ticked the home base option.

Your panel state (checkboxes, toggles, selections, text fields) is saved to your profile automatically and restored the next time you open Start Job, even from a different device. The saved state expires after 5 days.

🔧 Parts history — search past interventions

Every time you click Generate Gmail drafts, the parts and tools you entered are automatically saved to a shared database, indexed by squawk description, aircraft type, serial number, and Work Order number. This builds a searchable history of parts used across all past interventions by all engineers on the team.

To use it, go to the Parts & tools list section inside Start Job, then use the 🔍 Search squawk history bar at the top of the table. You can search in two ways:

  • Dropdown search: select one of your current work items — the system compares its keywords and meaning against all saved entries and shows the best matches, even if worded differently.
  • Free-text search: type any keyword or describe the issue naturally to find entries across all past jobs regardless of your current squawk list.

The results popup shows: squawk text, aircraft type, serial number, Work Order (FAXXXXX), engineer who entered the data, number of times those parts were used, and last date used. Results are split into Same aircraft type (highest confidence) and Other aircraft types (collapsed by default — always verify P/N applicability before using). Tick the parts you want and click ✓ Add selected to parts list to insert them directly into the table.

Important: the history is a starting point only. Always cross-check any suggested part number against the current AMM, IPC, and applicable release documentation before ordering or installing.

📬 Reply Sync — tracking Gmail responses

Reply Sync needs its own persistent Gmail connection (separate from the one-off Google sign-in used for "Draft in Gmail"), so it can keep scanning your threads in the background. Tap "🔗 Connect my Gmail (Reply Sync)" in the engineer bar (or Start Job header) once — it stays connected until Google requires re-authorisation (every 7 days while the app is in Google test mode), at which point the button turns into "⚠️ Reconnect Gmail".

Tap the "📬 Replies" button (in the main action row, or in the Start Job panel header) to see what Materials, the FBO, and the customer have replied on this job's Customer Update, Part Request and FBO Coordination email threads. Each of the three sections shows cached results instantly, then refreshes quietly in the background — or tap "🔄 Refresh now" / "🔄 Refresh all" to force it.

Each reply shows an AI-generated one-line summary, who it's from, and — where relevant — tappable buttons to apply what was found straight into your project:

  • Customer Update: your ETA on site, next scheduled flight, the customer's job/PO reference, and A/C hours & cycles (which then pre-fill every GA3671 tag for this job).
  • Part Request: Materials PO confirmation, and per-part status (confirmed, backordered, shipped, delivered, alternative P/N offered, tracking numbers) — matched automatically against your existing parts list where possible. A colleague adding or removing a part in the thread also shows up here for you to confirm.
  • FBO Coordination: shipping address, readiness date, contact name, and closed dates.
  • Any category: a "📝 Note in update" button lets you insert a mentioned part/shipment ETA directly into a specific work item's note for today's Daily Update.

If a reply isn't being found, add the exact subject wording your team uses for that email type in the "Can't find a reply?" box — it's shared with the whole team and improves detection for every future job with that wording.

☁️ Cloud sync & archives

All project data is saved to Firebase in real time and is accessible from any device — the ☁️ Synced badge in the header confirms your latest changes are saved (it briefly shows "⏳ Saving…" while a save is in progress). Mark a project as RTS to move it to Archives — it stays fully accessible but is separated from your active projects list.

Use the 🔄 button on any project card to transfer that project (aircraft info, work items, daily updates, parts) to a colleague's account — it disappears from your list and appears in theirs. Parts orders and Reply Sync history stay linked to the Work Order number, so nothing is lost.

📱 Install on your phone

Android (Chrome): open the site, tap the ⋮ menu, then "Add to Home screen" or "Install app".

iPhone (Safari): open the site, tap the Share icon, then "Add to Home Screen".

Once installed, the app opens full-screen from your home screen, just like a native app. Biometric login works once installed on both platforms.

🔒 Security

Data is stored in Firebase Realtime Database with authentication — only verified users (admin + engineers via PIN or biometric) can access it. Biometric login uses your device's secure enclave — no biometric data is ever sent to the server. PDF and email extraction is processed through a private Cloudflare Worker — your API key is never exposed in the app. All traffic is encrypted (HTTPS).

Your PIN is never stored or transmitted in plain text. A security question (set the first time you choose your PIN) lets you reset it yourself if forgotten, without contacting your admin. 5 incorrect PIN attempts lock your profile for 15 minutes. You're automatically signed out after 45 minutes of inactivity.
